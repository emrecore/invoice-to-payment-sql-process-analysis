# Business Case: Invoice-to-Payment Process Analysis

## Background

Invoice processing is a central operational process within Accounts Payable. Before an invoice can be paid, it must typically be received, validated, matched with a purchase order, approved by the responsible department, scheduled for payment, and processed by Accounting.

In real business environments, this process often includes exceptions such as:

* Missing purchase orders
* Price mismatches
* Quantity mismatches
* Missing approvals
* Duplicate invoice concerns
* Incorrect vendor master data

These exceptions can create manual work, increase processing times, delay payments, and reduce transparency across Finance and Procurement.

## Business Problem

The company does not have a clear, data-driven overview of its Invoice-to-Payment process performance.

Management needs to understand:

* Which process steps create the longest delays
* Which invoices require manual intervention
* Which vendors are linked to recurring exceptions
* Which departments are associated with slow approval or processing times
* Which invoices remain open and create financial exposure
* Whether exception cases take longer than standard invoice processes
* Which process areas should be prioritized for improvement

Without structured process analysis, bottlenecks and recurring operational issues can remain unnoticed.

## Project Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze the Invoice-to-Payment process using SQL and identify operational improvement opportunities.

The analysis focuses on:

* Invoice processing duration
* Payment timing and open invoice exposure
* Exception frequency and resolution time
* Vendor-related process performance
* Department-level bottlenecks
* Standard and exception process variants
* Data quality issues affecting process reliability

## Process Scope

The project analyzes a simplified Accounts Payable workflow:

```text
Invoice Received
→ Data Validation
→ PO Matching
→ Approval
→ Payment Scheduled
→ Paid
```

Invoices with exceptions may follow an extended process path:

```text
Invoice Received
→ Data Validation
→ Exception Raised
→ Correction Received or Correction Requested
→ PO Matching
→ Approval
→ Payment Scheduled
→ Paid
```

## Stakeholders

Relevant stakeholders include:

* Accounts Payable teams, responsible for invoice processing and payment execution
* Procurement teams, responsible for purchase order quality and vendor coordination
* Department managers, responsible for approvals and cost ownership
* Finance management, responsible for payment control and financial exposure
* Vendor management teams, responsible for supplier performance and master data quality

## Analytical Questions

The SQL analysis is designed to answer questions such as:

* How long does invoice processing take from receipt to payment scheduling?
* Which process transitions have the longest average duration?
* Which invoices have the longest end-to-end cycle time?
* Are exception processes slower than standard processes?
* Which exception types create the highest operational impact?
* Which vendors are associated with frequent exceptions or long cycle times?
* Which departments are linked to long approval waiting times?
* How many invoices remain open and what is their total value?
* Which payments were completed after their scheduled payment date?
* Which process cases should be prioritized for improvement?

## Key Assumptions

This project uses a synthetic dataset designed to represent a simplified but realistic Accounts Payable environment.

The model assumes:

* Each invoice belongs to one vendor and one responsible department
* Each invoice may optionally be linked to a purchase order
* A purchase order can be linked to multiple invoices
* Each invoice has one payment record in the current model
* Process performance is measured using timestamps from `invoice_events`
* Internal processing SLA is defined as payment scheduling within seven days after invoice receipt
* The department responsible for a process event is treated as the operational owner of that event
* Invoice and purchase order differences are treated as review cases rather than automatic data errors

## Expected Business Value

The analysis supports process optimization by helping management identify:

* Bottlenecks in invoice validation, matching, approval, and payment preparation
* Vendor relationships associated with high effort or recurring exceptions
* Exception types that cause significant delays
* Open invoice exposure requiring follow-up
* Opportunities to standardize workflows and reduce manual rework

The project demonstrates how SQL can transform operational transaction data into structured process insights for Finance and Procurement decision-making.
